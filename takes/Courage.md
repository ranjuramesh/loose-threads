"Everyone wants to be successful" - This statement is one of the universal truths for me with all the connotations involved. I would like to explore this further more atomically, but for now, let's stick to this. Every action of ours is towards being successful. There might be difference with direction or magnitude, but I would say this is a truthful statement. Personally, I am obsessed with courage. It comes from the fact that I believe I am not a very courageous person. So I have researching about courage for a long time and it has led to many nooks and crannies. This led me to read a lot and listen to lot of podcasts. But everything I did, still led my obsession to increase rather than decrease.

## What is courage?

"The quality of mind that enables us to act in the face danger, difficulty or death, inspite of possibility of pain, fear, failure or hesitation."

Lot of definitions that I have read, tries to portray courage to be a special property that needs a person to be fearless or exceptional. **I disagree**. Courage is all about taking action even though you don't have that special feeling of being fearless or brave. This brings a particular point to my mind - "When you act even when you are fearful, are you fearless? When you act even when you are not motivated, are you inspired?" I have not been able to answer this myself since it feels more like playing semantic games. It's left to each individual person to think further based on their preferences.

The way I have come to understand courage is through various stories that were part of my life and these are stories that I want to share.

## Story 1 - Born to be a founder

Varun is one of the people who has inspired me a lot. For me, he is one of the best examples of courage and it's evident in the way that he has led his life. Varun was born to a middle class family and he grew up helping his family in a clothing store and no particular inspiration in the vicinity of founding a tech startup. But he was brilliant in the way he made his way to Nanyang Tech in Singapore and inspite of being poached into Microsoft, eventually fought to start his first startup in the space of social tech. What most people don't know is that, he used to go to hackathons to win prize money when his company didn't have money to pay the employees. 

Here is the first question I keep pondering - "Varun could have joined a tech giant and made a lot of money with his technical brilliance anytime he wanted. But he persevered with the idea of building his own company for more than 8 years. Why?" The only answer I got was courage. Even though you can very easily live a very comfortable, blessed, peaceful life, you showcase the uncanny capability to ignore smoke and mirrors and fight day in and day out to build a company which is going to be a unicorn soon and one of the companies that I admire a lot.

## Story 2 - Instinct of a hero

Krishnan is one of my dearest friends and I have admired him for a long time. It was 2014 and Krishnan and Me had gone to Penang with family for a vacation. We ended up in the "Black sand beach" and were enjoying the sunset. Suddenly, there was a person who had strayed a bit too deep and was about to drown. Even before I could register what was happening, Krishnan had already jumped into the water and along with few other kind people, helped the man from drowning.

Here is the question I keep pondering - "Krishnan could have taken a few moments like everybody else to find the best way possible to save the person who is going to drown. Why did he jump in head first and save him?" The only answer I got was courage. Krishnan wasn't a great swimmer but he had the courage to act before things became worse and ended up saving a man.

## Story 3 - Pillar of the family

Raghu is my brother and is the one person I am closest to. After he completed his 10th standard, he could have easily gone to 11th and 12th and then gotten a degree. But our family was going through very tough financial situation. He took it upon himself to start working so that I could continue studying and our family's situation can improve.

Here is the question I keep pondering - "Raghu could have taken the natural path and left it to my parents to solve our financial difficulties and like every other 16 year old teenagers, chosen to go to college. But he choose to bear adulthood for the sake of family. Why did he sacrifice his youth?" The only answer I got was courage. Raghu had the courage to take the most difficult path which would have adverse impact on his future but still ended up building a business which now does a sizeable turnover and I couldn't be proud.

## Story 4 - Born to be a soldier

Captain Vikram Batra was my hero during my college days. His punch line - "Dil maange more" after winning a death combat while capture point 5140 of Tololing, he uttered them before breathing his last. He is the immortal hero who is awarded Param Veer Chakra and for a youngsters like in early 2000's, he became the ultimate beacon of supreme sacrifice. 

Here is the question I keep pondering - "Captain Vikram Batra could have played on our numerical superiority and eventually captured the point, which is strategically acceptable. But he still chose to capture it with minimum casualty, but gave his life instead. Why?" The only answer I got was courage. Captain Vikram Batra had the courage to give his life up rather than sacrifice the soldiers he commanded and didn't even think twice.

## Story 5 - Obsessed with service

Ashok was my batchmate in degree college and came from a very poor family. He barely had any money to pay for this day to day expenses and did so by doing odd chores all the while studying for degree. But the most extraordinary thing about him was that he was obsessed with becoming an army officer. He had trained for 6 years to become an army office and went to "Tal Sainik Camp" of NCC twice. TSC is one of the toughest competitions in NCC and requires superhuman perseverance to get through. He did that twice. Unfortunately, he failed his CDSE exams, but kept trying for the next 3 years until his age limit was reached. All the while, he used to do journalism on the side which gave him barely enough to survive.

Here is the question I keep pondering - "Ashok could have redirected his super human focus on any other thing and he would have gotten out of his poverty very easily. But he chose to go after his dream of "Service to his motherland". He kept at it for 9 years in total, until he was forcefully made to stop. Why?" The only answer I got was courage. Ashok would have given up even more of himself to go after what was more important to him.

## Conclusion

There are many more stories that I could keep writing about, but the point about stories is that it's only understood by people who relate to the circumstances. But the all these stories convey what I am trying to say very clearly - "Courage is the source of greatness."
Courage gives you ability to act upon opportunities that others run away from. Courage lets you stand up for what you believe in. Courage gives the ability to grab the opportunity that can change your life.
My only advice is to be aware of yourself and keep acting upon your beliefs.
