### 💥 The Lead Story: SpaceX Makes Its $60B Move on Cursor

This is the deal everyone's talking about this morning.

SpaceX has struck a $10 billion collaboration deal with AI coding startup Cursor — with an option to acquire the company outright for $60 billion later this year. The deal pairs Cursor's AI coding expertise with SpaceX's Colossus supercomputer.

Until a few hours before the announcement, Cursor was on track to close a $2 billion funding round valuing it at $50 billion. SpaceX preempted that round entirely. The acquisition itself is being delayed until after SpaceX's IPO this summer — making it easier to finance the $60 billion purchase using publicly traded stock.

The competitive angle: whether it's a temporary team-up or the start of an acquisition, the arrangement strengthens a direct competitor to Anthropic's Claude Code and OpenAI's Codex. Cursor's CEO noted they've been "bottlenecked by compute" — xAI's Colossus solves that.

The SpaceX IPO, targeted for June, is aiming for a $1.75 to $1.8 trillion valuation, potentially the largest in history.

### 🏗️ The Infrastructure Arms Race Intensifies

This is the macro story underneath everything right now. Product launches, startup valuations, cloud deals, and chip shortages all sit on top of an unprecedented infrastructure buildout. AI is no longer just a software cycle — it is a capital cycle.

Amazon announced a $5 billion investment in Anthropic this week, giving the AI startup broader access to its Trainium chips. The hyperscaler-to-AI-lab pipeline is becoming a defining structure of the industry.

Meanwhile, Barron's reported that investors are paying closer attention to Google's next-generation TPUs as a real competitive threat to Nvidia, even as analysts still argue Nvidia retains a strong lead.

### 🕵️ Meta Turns Its Employees Into Training Data

Meta is installing tracking software on US employees' work computers that will capture mouse movements, clicks, and keystrokes, along with screenshots — to feed the data into its AI training pipeline. The tool was disclosed in a memo to staff in a channel belonging to the Meta Superintelligence Labs team.

This follows Meta's Muse Spark launch and signals how seriously they're trying to close the gap with OpenAI and Anthropic. It'll also generate serious privacy blowback.

### 🏛️ The Policy Battlefield Opens Up

Anthropic has scaled up its Washington lobbying operations significantly. The policy contest is now part of the product contest — when frontier labs scale their political operations, it means the regulatory decisions ahead affect contracts, procurement, export controls, model access, and business models.

On surveillance: with Section 702 authorities nearing expiration, lawmakers are split over whether to renew US surveillance powers or overhaul them. The most contentious issue is a loophole allowing agencies to buy Americans' commercial data from brokers and use AI models to analyze vast location datasets without a warrant.

### 🧠 The Snowflake Pivot Worth Watching

Snowflake is betting that the future of AI isn't just analyzing data — it's acting on it. That means a shift away from chatbots and toward autonomous agents that can actually get work done. For a data platform company, this is a significant strategic repositioning and signals how every enterprise software vendor is scrambling to become an "agent" play.
